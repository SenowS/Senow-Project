In League of Legends, one of the most impactful things on the game is how one handles the laning phase. This could be anything from how you would roam into the enemy team's jungle, help out with your own jungle or just help out the other lanes. No champion is more capable of this than Ahri. Though some would say she has a weaker laning phase, she more than makes up for it with her ability to be mobile and help out throughout the map. Below I will include a picture of the runes/spells that you will take in all matchups.

Now that you have chosen your starting items the fun starts. Before you are level 6 you will need to play safer. So you will want to be sitting just out of range of your opponent but close enough that you can poke them with your abilities. At first sight of them rushing at you, you will need to back away. But with this safe playstyle it will enable you to watch your map to be able to help your team whenever you see the opening. One strong recommendation is always moving to the side that your jungler is sitting on, so that they may assist you or you may help them as soon as something arises.

Abilities in lane are often up for debate by the community. A very consistent and strong order of leveling will be included in the picture below:

As the lane continues, you will be earning gold from killing the minions or just as time goes. Items play just as important of a role, now the order of when to buy items can be situational. But a rough idea of what order to build in should be as follows:

1. Everfrost (Your mythical item that slows or roots enemies)
2. Sorcerer’s Shoes (For the magic penetration and movement speed)
3. Shadowflame (For extra magic penetration and ability power)
4. A. Zhonya’s Hourglass (If you need more defensive stats)
4. B. Rabadon’s Deathcap (If you need higher ability power)
5. Whichever option you didn’t take from “4”
6. Void Staff (Significant boost to your magic penetration) This one if needed can be purchased as a 4th item but doesn’t carry the priority that the other two have.

Something that can’t be stressed enough is your vision control!! There are things called “wards” that light up the map for not only you but your team as well. The ideal places for these wards are anywhere that are considered “high traffic” areas. The most important wards are the red ones, they only cost 75g and are permanent. These will only disappear if your opponent can find them and destroy them. By using these wards in an effective way, not only will you and your team be safe but it will make it much easier to predict your opponent's moves.

Once you make it to level 6, the world opens up to you. Now is when you want to be looking into showing up in other peoples lanes to help them out. This is known as roaming and it is something Ahri can do quite well. With her ultimate, it makes things much easier for you to just chase down your opponent and secure your team kills and just overall pressure. Ideally, the bottom lane should be #1 on the list of priorities. Mainly due to the fact that there are two of your teammates and two opponents down there.

These are the basics to how to survive as Ahri is the laning phase. In the future I will release a more advanced guide that will go further into depth about all the different strategies or options you can take to further improve at laning.

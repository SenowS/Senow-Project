Keeping it safe with Shaun

Written by Shaun Senow, March 13th 2022

When it comes to online security, one of the most unfortunate things has to be when your account gets hacked. This can come from a variety of reasons, but the primary one has to be an unsecured unsecure password. Now most people's passwords are far from secure, this is in part due to either their laziness or no creativity. I will be listing a few things that can help you secure your passwords. There are many more suggestions but these are the most basic steps to help keep up.

  1. Use special characters! When it comes to spelling out a password, opt-in opt in for the special characters replacing common letters. E’s become 3’s and S’s become $s. It makes the process of guessing your password a whole lot harder.
  2. DON’T USE THE SAME PASSWORD!! I can’t stress this one enough. One password for every website will for sure lead to your account being hacked into. All it takes is one site to be compromised in any way, then the hackers will have all they need to get into everything.
  3. Change your passwords every 3-6 months. Having your passwords constantly rotating or evolving, will further reduce the chances of being hacked. If you want to take it a step further you can change them every month.
  4. Avoid using words that are easily predictable. Everyone loves their pets and children, but using their names in your password makes things that much easier to figure out. Same with your birthday or anniversary years. If you insist on using words in your passwords, I would highly recommend picking random words that have no relation to yourself. Even with the numbers, you may throw into there. Using a random number generator will yield far stronger results.

Keeping up to date with your passwords is a very important thing to do. At times it can be annoying to have to remember so many but the cons of not changing them far outweigh outweighs the pros. One small slip in your passwords could result in someone getting access to something very important. In one of the worst-case worst case scenarios, it could be your bank info. I don’t need to explain how bad that could be.

Trying to keep up to date could be a challenge but there are options out there. You could go with the classic sticky note play but something that would be much better would be a password manager. They are one of the safest ways to store and use your passwords, obviously, they aren’t 100% foolproof fool proof but they most certainly work. Many managers use layer after layer of security to keep things safe and locked in. Very rarely do you ever hear of one being compromised?.

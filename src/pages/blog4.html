Beginners Guide to Don't Starve

Written by Shaun Senow, April 14th 2022

One of the most challenging games has to be Don’t Starve. Now if you agree with that statement and want to learn how to start out, you have come to the right place! In a game where everything is out to kill you and it most certainly finds a way to do just that. In this short and simple guide, I will be explaining how a first year should go which is a great start to being able to survive in this harsh world.

Days 1 - 6:

When it comes to your first few days, you are mainly just going to be collecting grass and sticks. LOTS OF THEM!! Use these to make basic tools such as the pickaxe, torch and axe. While collecting, try and take note of any beefalos you may find and ultimately you want to be searching for the pig village. A side bonus would be finding the chester or clockwork creatures. When it becomes dark, stick to using just a torch to make it through the night. If they are around the area, try and mine the rocks that show up which have a gold vein in them.

Days 7 - 14:

Now that you have collected as many basic resources that you can find, it is time to set up your camp. Try and avoid things that will be hostile to you. This being swamps, ponds or anywhere that there are spiders. Some basic things to build in this base is of course a firepit for the long nights, a science machine so that you may have access to better tools and an alchemy engine to further improve on your tools.

After these necessary things have been set up, you are going to want to look towards food production. Making many farming plots is essential to being able to survive with how diverse they are. On top of these, it’s safe to assume that you may need to build some crock-pots to cook those vegetables and meat. Finally to top off your food area, it’s going to need some drying racks. Drying meat not only makes them last longer, but also offers some quick health on the go.

Days 15 - Winter:

If you have made it this far without too many problems, consider yourself lucky. But unfortunately things will only get harder. At this point you’ve probably realized that food spoils quicker than one might expect. Simple solution to this is turning to the refrigerator. During the first few days while exploring, you may have come across the clockwork creatures. Now is the time to go and kill them, so you are going to want to make some log suits to use as armor and spear. Once these are dead, it’s time to make an icebox which is used to store your food to preserve it.

From this point until winter comes, just start stocking up on everything food and resource related. If there is some extra time to kill, one great option is to go out and try to find some spiders to kill for some silk and monster meat. While searching for these, if you can find a hut area, it will have the walrus guy show up in winter. By killing him you may be able to get his tools that will greatly improve your life.

Winter:

With winter now upon you, there is not a whole lot more to say. Something that is crucial to making it through is using the food that you have stored as well as making a heat stone to keep you warm and not freeze to death. If the option is there, try and make a winter hat to further ensure that you do not succumb to the cold.

The only real thing to do in the winter aside from trying not to freeze or starve is trying to kill that walrus which was previously mentioned. Using a boomerang is your best bet to kill him or if they are around and you can afford it, feed the pigs that are around your pig village. If they die while fighting you can cook them and eat them, so really it’s a win win.

There you have it! With this guide, you are all set to survive your first year in Don’t Starve. The following years will get much harder, but you are on the right track. Check back in the future for another guide on how to make it through the following years and maybe even how to survive in the underground.
